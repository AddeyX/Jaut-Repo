The short answer? **Yes.** In the web design world, the "barefoot cobbler" (a dev with no website) is a red flag. If you are selling digital presence and professional "social proof," but you don't have a digital home yourself, it creates a massive trust gap.

However, as a startup bootstrapper, you must avoid the "Portfolio Trap"—spending three months perfecting your own site while ignoring actual paying clients.

Here is the rational, high-impact reasoning for why you need a site and what it should actually accomplish:

### 1. The "Dogfooding" Principle

If you are pitching a "High-Performance Svelte/Cloudflare" solution that beats WordPress on speed, your own site is your primary **Proof of Concept**.

* **The Test:** When you send a lead magnet, the first thing a prospect will do is click your link. If your site doesn't load instantly, your pitch is dead.
* **The Reality:** Your site is your "Demo." It should be a living example of the exact "Chassis" you are selling to them.

### 2. Eliminating Friction (The Onboarding Gateway)

Your site shouldn't just be a "brochure." It should be a **functional tool** in your assembly line.

* It hosts your **Tally onboarding form**.
* It provides the **Knowledge Base** for your support.
* It serves as the "Hub" where you send people to pay their Lemon Squeezy deposits.
* Without a site, you are stuck sending messy, manual links in emails, which feels like a "side-hustle" rather than a "Digital Partner."

### 3. SEO and Authority

Even if you start with cold outreach, you eventually want "Warm Leads" finding you.

* By having a site that is optimized for "Web Design for Contractors in [Your Area]" or "Fastest Sites for Independent Coaches," you start building **long-term organic equity**.
* It’s much easier to sell a $1,500 "Partner" tier when you have a professional `.com` that looks like it belongs to a $5k+ agency.

---

### The "Lean" Way to Do It (Avoid Over-Engineering)

Don't build a 20-page monster. For a bootstrapper, a **Single-Page Powerhouse** is enough to get your first 10 clients.

**What your site needs (The "Essential 4"):**

1. **The "Hero" Hook:** "I build high-performance digital assets for [Niche] that load 4x faster than WordPress."
2. **The Comparison:** A side-by-side speed test (Your Stack vs. A typical DIY builder).
3. **The Pricing Tiers:** Transparency builds trust. Show them exactly what they are getting.
4. **The "Start Now" Button:** This should link directly to your Tally "Content Gateway."

---

### The "Failure" Warning: Don't get stuck in "The Loop"

Many agencies fail because the founder spends weeks tweaking their own CSS instead of cold-calling. **Set a hard 6-hour limit** to build your own site using your "Chassis." If you can't build your own site in a day, your "Assembly Line" isn't ready for clients yet.
